Airports in Malaysia

Large airports are concentrated in key areas like Kuala Lumpur, emphasizing their role in national and international air traffic. Medium and small airports are distributed more evenly throughout the country, supporting regional connectivity. Heliports and balloonports are less prevalent, serving specialized transportation needs.

Large airports, like Kuala Lumpur International Airport, are strategically located at lower elevations to accommodate higher air traffic volume efficiently. Lower elevations make it easier for larger aircraft to take off and land, which is essential for airports handling higher traffic, both international and domestic. Small and medium airports, located across varying elevations, serve regional or local travel, focusing more on connectivity than on high-volume operations.

Small airports make up the largest portion of airports in Malaysia because they are essential for connecting rural or less populated areas. These airports handle mostly domestic flights or serve as hubs for smaller regions, supporting local economies and improving accessibility. They require less infrastructure and investment compared to large international airports, making them more feasible in remote locations.

Sabah and Sarawak, the top two regions with 35 and 32 airports respectively, have more airports due to their large land areas and challenging terrains, which require more air travel to connect remote regions. In contrast, Putrajaya and Labuan have fewer airports due to their smaller size and proximity to major airports like Kuala Lumpur International Airport, which efficiently serves their transportation needs.
